### Combined Review of Non-Promotional Materials for CholeCap

#### Compliance with Non-Promotional Material Guidelines

**Guideline Sources:**
- EFPIA (European Federation of Pharmaceutical Industries and Associations) Codes and Guidelines
- Communicate With Care Pocket Guide
- Disclaimer Texts for Non-promotional Material

### Review Points and Suggested Changes:

1. **Original Sentence:**
   "The first and only statin approved for the treatment of hypercholesterolaemia in teenagers with familial hypercholesterolaemia¹."
   
   **Deviation:**
   - Emphasizes uniqueness, potentially seen as promotional.
   - According to [EFPIA Chapter 3, Article 17], educational content must not serve as an inducement to recommend a product.
   
   **Combined New Sentence:**
   "CholeCap is among the statins approved for the treatment of hypercholesterolaemia in teenagers with familial hypercholesterolaemia."
   
   **Explanation:**
   This revision removes the implied superiority and ensures informational accuracy without promotional undertones.

2. **Original Sentence:**
   "Clinically proven efficacy and safety in paediatric patients²."
   
   **Deviation:**
   - Lacks context and specific data.
   - Should be clearly fact-based as per [Communicate With Care Pocket Guide: Article 2.iii].
   
   **Combined New Sentence:**
   "Clinical studies have demonstrated efficacy and safety in managing hypercholesterolaemia in paediatric patients, with detailed results available in published studies.²"
   
   **Explanation:**
   This revision maintains factual accuracy and provides necessary context, ensuring clarity and compliance.

3. **Original Sentence:**
   "Dolor sit amet."
   
   **Deviation:**
   - Placeholder text, lacking necessary disclaimers.
   - Must include appropriate disclaimers as per [Disclaimer Texts for Non-Promotional Material: Article 3].
   
   **Combined New Sentence:**
   "[Disclaimer: The image shown is a model and not a real patient. The prescription example shown is not based on a real patient.]"
   
   **Explanation:**
   Adding relevant disclaimers provides clarity and aligns with guidelines.

4. **Original Sentence:**
   "Reduction in LDL-C²."
   
   **Deviation:**
   - Lacks context and balance.
   - Should present information in a balanced manner as per [EFPIA Chapter 3: Article 16].
   
   **Combined New Sentence:**
   "Study results demonstrate a reduction in LDL-C levels over time with CholeCap treatment.²"
   
   **Explanation:**
   This provides more context and adheres to guidelines for balanced, evidence-based content.

5. **Original Sentence:**
   "49% Reduction in LDL-C at week 24 compared to baseline."
   
   **Deviation:**
   - Needs evidence-based context to avoid off-label promotion.
   - According to [Global Legal & Compliance Pocket Guide: Article 1], such information must be balanced and objective.
   
   **Combined New Sentence:**
   "Research data shows a 49% reduction in LDL-C levels at week 24 compared to baseline, indicating significant management of hypercholesterolaemia."
   
   **Explanation:**
   This ensures that the data is presented accurately and contextually, avoiding promotional implications.

6. **Original Sentence:**
   "CholeCap significantly reduces elevated total cholesterol, LDL-C, and apo B levels in boys and postmenarchal girls, 10 to 17 years of age, with heterozygous familial hypercholesterolaemia.¹"
   
   **Deviation:**
   - Strong claims about benefits could be promotional.
   - Must be purely educational as per [EFPIA Chapter 3: Article 17].
   
   **Combined New Sentence:**
   "CholeCap is utilized to manage elevated cholesterol levels in paediatric patients with familial hypercholesterolaemia, with effects observed on total cholesterol, LDL-C, and apo B levels.¹"
   
   **Explanation:**
   This revision ensures a non-promotional and educational tone, presenting balanced information.

7. **Original Sentence:**
   "Welcome to a brighter future for your patients."
   
   **Deviation:**
   - Could be interpreted as promotional.
   - According to [EFPIA Chapter 4: Article 21], interactions should be transparent and non-promotional.
   
   **Combined New Sentence:**
   "Consult current guidelines for managing patients with hypercholesterolaemia to ensure best practices."
   
   **Explanation:**
   This sentence provides actionable information and maintains a neutral, informative tone.

### Conclusion:
By combining and enhancing the suggestions from both reviews, the revised document now aligns with the Non-Promotional Material Guidelines. It maintains an informative and factual tone, ensuring clarity and compliance with global standards for non-promotional communications in healthcare.
